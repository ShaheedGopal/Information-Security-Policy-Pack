# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to define the appropriate use of the organization's information systems and technology resources.

This policy aims to:
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of company information  
- Reduce security risks and prevent misuse of systems  
- Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations  
- Promote responsible and secure user behavior  

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees  
- Contractors and consultants  
- Temporary staff and interns  
- Third parties with access to company systems or data  

The policy covers all technology resources, including:
- Computers, laptops, and mobile devices  
- Networks and internet access  
- Email and communication systems  
- Cloud services and applications  
- Data and information assets  

---

## 3. Definitions

**Technology Resources**  
All IT systems, devices, software, and networks owned or managed by the organization.

**Company Data**  
Any data created, processed, or stored by the organization, including confidential, internal, and public information.

---

## 4. Acceptable Use Principles
Users must:
- Use technology resources only for legitimate business purposes  
- Act responsibly and ethically when accessing company systems  
- Comply with all company policies and applicable laws  
- Protect company data from unauthorized access or disclosure  

---

## 5. User Responsibilities
All users are responsible for:
- Safeguarding login credentials and passwords  
- Not sharing accounts or authentication details  
- Locking devices when unattended  
- Using strong passwords and complying with the Password Policy  
- Reporting suspicious emails, phishing attempts, or security incidents  
- Protecting sensitive information at all times  

---

## 6. Prohibited Activities
Users must NOT:
- Access systems without authorization  
- Share passwords or credentials  
- Install unauthorized software or hardware  
- Download or distribute pirated or illegal content  
- Attempt to bypass or disable security controls  
- Use company systems for illegal, fraudulent, or unethical activities  
- Engage in harassment, discrimination, or offensive behavior  

---

## 7. Data Protection and Information Handling
Users must:
- Handle data according to its classification (e.g., Confidential, Internal, Public)  
- Encrypt sensitive data when transmitted externally  
- Avoid storing company data on unauthorized devices or platforms  
- Ensure data is only shared with authorized individuals  
- Comply with applicable data protection laws (e.g., POPIA, GDPR)  

---

## 8. System and Device Usage

### 8.1 Company Devices
- Must be used primarily for business purposes  
- Must have approved security controls installed (e.g., antivirus, updates)  
- Must not be altered, tampered with, or misused  

### 8.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
- Must be approved before accessing company systems  
- Must comply with security requirements (e.g., encryption, PIN/password protection)  
- Must not store sensitive data without proper controls  

---

## 9. Internet and Email Usage

### Internet Usage
Users must NOT:
- Access malicious, illegal, or offensive websites  
- Download unsafe or unauthorized content  

### Email Usage
Users must:
- Use email responsibly and professionally  
- Avoid sending sensitive data without encryption  
- Not send spam, chain messages, or malicious content  

---

## 10. Access Control
- Access must follow the **principle of least privilege**  
- Users may only access information necessary for their role  
- Privileged accounts must be strictly controlled and monitored  
- Access rights must not be shared or transferred  

---

## 11. Monitoring and Privacy
The organization reserves the right to:
- Monitor system usage, network activity, and communications  
- Log user activity for security and compliance purposes  

Users should have **no expectation of privacy** when using company systems.

---

## 12. Incident Reporting
All users must immediately report:
- Suspected security incidents  
- Data breaches or leaks  
- Lost or stolen devices  
- Unauthorized access attempts  

Reports should be made to:
- IT Security Team  
- Service Desk  
- Line Manager  

---

## 13. Third-Party and Vendor Usage
- Third-party access must be approved and controlled  
- Vendors must comply with organizational security standards  
- Contracts must include confidentiality and security requirements  

---

## 14. Compliance
Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action  
- Revocation of system access  
- Termination of employment or contract  
- Legal action where applicable  

---

## 15. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Users | Follow this policy and protect systems |
| IT Team | Enforce technical controls |
| Security Team | Monitor compliance and manage risks |
| Management | Ensure awareness and enforcement |

---

## 16. Exceptions
Any exceptions to this policy:
- Must be formally requested  
- Must be documented and justified  
- Must be approved by management and the Security Team  

---

## 17. Review and Maintenance
This policy will be:
- Reviewed annually  
- Updated based on regulatory, technological, or business changes
