
# Password Policy

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Password Policy is to define strong authentication practices to safeguard access to systems, applications, and data. This policy aims to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, credential compromise, and security breaches.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff  
- All systems, applications, and services that require authentication  
- All user accounts, including standard, privileged, and service accounts  

---

## 3. Definitions

**Password**  
A secret string of characters used to authenticate a user.

**Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**  
A security mechanism requiring two or more authentication factors (e.g., password + one-time code).

**Privileged Account**  
An account with elevated permissions (e.g., admin, root access).

---

## 4. Password Requirements

All passwords must comply with the following:

- Minimum length: **12 characters**  
- Must include:
  - Uppercase letters (A–Z)  
  - Lowercase letters (a–z)  
  - Numbers (0–9)  
  - Special characters (e.g., !@#$%)  
- Must not contain:
  - Username or user ID  
  - Easily guessable words (e.g., "password", "123456")  
- Password reuse:
  - Last **5 passwords may not be reused**  
- Password expiry:
  - Passwords must be changed every **90 days** (unless MFA + risk-based controls apply)  

---

## 5. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

- MFA is **mandatory** for:
  - Privileged accounts  
  - Remote access (VPN, cloud systems)  
  - Access to sensitive or critical systems  

- MFA methods may include:
  - One-time passwords (OTP)  
  - Authentication apps  
  - Hardware tokens  

---

## 6. Password Management

### 6.1 Storage
- Passwords must never be stored in plain text  
- Systems must securely hash and protect passwords  

### 6.2 Transmission
- Passwords must not be transmitted unencrypted  
- Secure protocols (HTTPS, VPN) must be used  

### 6.3 Sharing
- Password sharing is strictly prohibited  
- Shared accounts must be avoided where possible  

---

## 7. User Responsibilities

All users are responsible for:

- Keeping passwords confidential  
- Not reusing passwords across personal and work accounts  
- Locking devices when unattended  
- Immediately reporting suspected password compromise  
- Following all password and authentication requirements  

---

## 8. Best Practices

Users should:

- Use a reputable **password manager** to generate and store passwords  
- Create **unique passwords** for each system  
- Avoid writing passwords down or storing them in unsecured locations  
- Enable MFA wherever available  
- Be cautious of phishing attempts and social engineering  

---

## 9. Account Lockout and Session Management

- Accounts will be locked after **5 failed login attempts**  
- Locked accounts must be re-enabled through **IT Service Desk or Security Team**  
- Session timeouts must be enforced after periods of inactivity  
- Re-authentication may be required for sensitive actions  

---

## 10. Privileged Account Controls

- Privileged accounts must:
  - Use stronger authentication controls (MFA mandatory)  
  - Be monitored and logged  
  - Be limited to authorized personnel only  
- Privileged access must follow the **least privilege principle**  
- Use of privileged accounts must be audited regularly  

---

## 11. Monitoring and Enforcement

The organization reserves the right to:

- Monitor authentication activity  
- Detect and respond to suspicious login behavior  
- Enforce password policies through technical controls  

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Account suspension or access revocation  
- Disciplinary action  
- Legal consequences where applicable  

---

## 12. Incident Reporting

Users must immediately report:

- Suspected password compromise  
- Unauthorized account access  
- Phishing or credential harvesting attempts  

Reports should be made to:
- IT Security Team  
- Service Desk  
- Line Manager  

---

## 13. Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:

- ISO/IEC 27001  
- POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act)  
- GDPR (where applicable)  
- Internal information security policies  

Failure to comply may result in enforcement actions as defined above.

---

## 14. Exceptions

- Exceptions must be formally requested  
- Must include business justification  
- Must be approved by IT Security and management  

---

- Reviewed annually
