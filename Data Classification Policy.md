
# Data Classification Policy

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Data Classification Policy is to establish a structured framework for classifying organizational data based on sensitivity and criticality, and to ensure that appropriate handling, storage, transmission, and disposal controls are applied.

This policy aims to:
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information
- Reduce the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access
- Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations (e.g., POPIA, GDPR)
- Promote consistent data handling practices across the organization

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties
- All organizational data, whether digital or physical
- All systems, devices, and storage locations used to process or store data

Data covered includes, but is not limited to:
- Customer and client data
- Financial information
- Employee records
- System and operational data
- Intellectual property

---

## 3. Definitions

**Data Classification**  
The process of categorizing data based on its sensitivity and required level of protection.

**Sensitive Data**  
Information that could cause harm to the organization or individuals if disclosed, altered, or lost.

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**  
Data that can identify an individual (e.g., ID numbers, names, contact details).

**PHI (Protected Health Information)**  
Health-related personal data protected under applicable regulations.

---

## 4. Data Classification Levels

### 4.1 Public
Information approved for public release.

**Examples:**
- Marketing materials
- Website content
- Public reports

---

### 4.2 Internal
Information intended for internal use only.

**Examples:**
- Internal emails
- Meeting notes
- Operational procedures

---

### 4.3 Confidential
Sensitive information requiring restricted access.

**Examples:**
- Employee records
- Financial data
- Business plans

---

### 4.4 Restricted
Highly sensitive information requiring strict control and monitoring.

**Examples:**
- Customer personal data (PII)
- Health records (PHI)
- Authentication credentials
- Encryption keys

---

## 5. Data Handling Requirements

| Classification | Storage | Transmission | Access Control | Disposal |
|---------------|--------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Public | Unrestricted | No encryption required | Public access | Standard disposal |
| Internal | Approved internal systems | Encrypted when external | Internal users only | Secure deletion or shredding |
| Confidential | Encrypted storage required | Encrypted transmission required | Authorized personnel only | Secure deletion or shredding |
| Restricted | Encrypted + monitored systems | Encrypted + access-controlled | Strictly limited access | Secure deletion + verification |

---

## 6. Data Handling Guidelines

### 6.1 Storage
- Data must only be stored in approved systems and locations
- Sensitive data must be encrypted at rest
- Unauthorized storage (e.g., personal devices, unapproved cloud platforms) is prohibited

---

### 6.2 Transmission
- Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted during transmission
- Secure channels (VPN, HTTPS, SFTP) must be used
- Emailing sensitive data must follow encryption and approval requirements

---

### 6.3 Access Control
- Access must follow the **principle of least privilege**
- Users must only access data required for their role
- Access rights must be reviewed periodically
- Privileged access must be monitored and logged

---

### 6.4 Data Sharing
- Data must only be shared with authorized individuals
- External sharing requires approval
- Confidentiality agreements must be in place where applicable

---

### 6.5 Disposal
- Data must be securely destroyed when no longer needed
- Physical documents must be shredded
- Electronic data must be securely deleted or wiped
- Disposal must be verified for sensitive data

---

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Employees | Classify and handle data appropriately |
| Managers | Ensure teams comply with policy |
| IT Team | Implement technical controls for data protection |
| Security Team | Monitor data usage and enforce compliance |
| Data Owners | Define classification and access requirements |

---

## 8. Data Ownership

- All data must have an assigned **Data Owner**
- Data Owners are responsible for:
  - Classifying data
  - Approving access
  - Ensuring proper handling

---

## 9. Compliance Requirements

This policy supports compliance with:
- ISO/IEC 27001
- POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act)
- GDPR (where applicable)
- Internal security and governance policies

Non-compliance may result in:
- Disciplinary action
- Loss of access privileges
- Legal or regulatory penalties

---

## 10. Monitoring and Enforcement

The organization reserves the right to:
- Monitor data access and usage
- Audit systems and storage locations
- Investigate policy violations

Violations will be:
- Logged and assessed
- Investigated by the Security or Compliance team
- Escalated where necessary

---

## 11. Incident Reporting

Users must immediately report:
- Data breaches or leaks
- Unauthorized access
- Loss or theft of devices containing company data

Reports must be made to:
- IT Security Team
- Service Desk
- Line Manager

---

## 12. Exceptions

- Any exceptions must be formally requested
- Must be documented with justification
- Must be approved by management and security

---

## 13. Review and Maintenance

This policy will be:
- Reviewed annually
- Updated based on regulatory or business changes
